Elara followed the map to the heart of the Whispering Woods, where she discovered an ancient tree whose roots guarded a hidden spring of shimmering water. The whispers revealed themselves as the voices of the forest, guiding her to restore the long-forgotten source of life that once nourished the land. When she cleared the roots and let the water flow freely again, the woods grew calm and welcoming, and the curse that had kept people away was lifted. Elara returned to Elderglen with the spring’s blessing, and soon the village flourished—fields grew greener, wells never ran dry, and the people spoke her name with gratitude, as the girl who listened when the forest called.


Elara reached the heart of the Whispering Woods just as the last light of day disappeared. Before her stood an ancient tree, hollow and weeping sap like tears, and beneath it the cursed spring pulsed with a dark, unnatural glow. The whispers that had once guided her now rose into a sorrowful chorus, revealing the truth too late—the map had not been a guide to salvation, but a lure for a new guardian to replace what the forest had lost long ago.

As Elara stepped closer, the ground gave way beneath her, and cold roots coiled around her like chains. She tried to call out, but the woods swallowed her voice, accepting her as its final offering. By morning, there was no trace of her path, only a new, faint whisper in the wind. In Elderglen, the prosperity she had hoped to bring never came, and the village slowly faded into memory, as if the forest had taken its story as well.
